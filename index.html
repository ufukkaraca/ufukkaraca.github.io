Experience and Education

Studying Electrical Engineering and Management Information Systems majors, I work on turning theory into practice by hands-on experiences and tech-focused projects. I'm a self-taught developer, and I work on developing beautiful things.

Hardware: I have developed an awarded wearable emergency-locating system, small-scale automation circuits and PoC systems for pipelining.

Software: I have designed numerous websites, developed mobile applications and handled the full-stack development for big data, management and HR programs.

Case: Piëch

As a 6-month intern at Piëch Automotive, I developed multiple systems utilizing numerous technologies. The company 'Hub' system, of which I ran the full-stack development, is comprised of over 18,000 code lines.

Including a PLM and HR applications, it enables the company to create real-time AR models from BOM data, in the meanwhile enabling all departments from finance to engineering to work on the most up-to-date data. It also enables real-time control over supplier management, access, and employee vacation management.

Side projects include blockchain prototyping, web parsing and more.

Which means...

I can do front-end, back-end or full-stack development, work on big data management and help manage complex development projects.

HAYAT 433
December 2017 - April 2018

HAYAT 433 was an ultra-affordable locating system designed to minimise the amount of time spent finding victims after hydrological and geomorphological hazards. It uses RF transmitters and receivers within the base of a wearable microchip to provide rescue teams information about distance and 'recoverability' of victims.
It was rewarded national first place in Science Expo Turkey 2018.
